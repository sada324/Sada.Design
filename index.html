About

How I work.

I don't stay in one lane. A sign is also a circuit board. A landing page is also the whole first impression of a business. I build the version that actually ships — no filler, no demo mode, no decoration that isn't doing something.

I design products, systems, and everything in between.

My work sits across industrial design, manufacturing, software, branding, and growth. I explore emerging technologies, changing behaviours, and new production methods to identify opportunities worth building around—then translate those signals into products, systems, and experiences that create greater value through thoughtful design.

I am drawn to ideas that are ambitious, practical, or highly specific, and that can be realized in novel ways. I focus on achieving quality without unnecessary production cost, creating genuine value, and finding thoughtful ways for new ideas to earn attention in an increasingly pay-to-play digital environment.

The goal is not to simplify the thinking, but to turn complex thinking into something clear, useful, and real.

SY 1 — In Development

SY Mark 1

The world's first AI-guided recumbent reverse trike. A modular aluminum tube frame, 3D-printed connectors, and zero welding — built to be assembled, repaired, and upgraded by the person who owns it.

The Frame

How it's built.

01
Modular Frame System
3D-printed connectors joined to aluminum tubes. No welding required, no specialized shop.
02
3D-Printed Connectors
High-strength PETG connectors with steel inserts, bolt-through by design.
03
Bolt-Together Assembly
Precision-fit parts. Basic tools to build, basic tools to repair.
04
Lightweight & Strong
Aluminum tube structure tuned for strength-to-weight, not just weight.
05
Easy Maintenance
Every component replaces or upgrades individually. Built for builders, not landfills.
06
Built for the Road
Minimalist, responsive, lightweight. Fun to drive — that's the whole spec.
The Thesis

Why it exists.

Most personal mobility is either a bicycle with no protection or a car with too much mass. SY Mark 1 sits in the gap — a low, stable, human-powered trike with an AI layer that guides the ride, built from a frame you can take apart with a hex key and put back together better than before.
